# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PreEdu EngKids is used by a volunteer facilitator operating a laptop in front of a small classroom. The learner audience is a mixed-age group of children, roughly kindergarten through elementary school, watching through a projector and answering aloud or taking turns at the laptop.

## Product Purpose

The product is an interactive English teaching tool for limited in-person sessions. It helps a facilitator introduce alphabet, vocabulary, pronunciation, and simple sentence patterns through clear slides, large visual prompts, mascot feedback, audio pronunciation, and short mini-games. Success means the facilitator can run the session smoothly, children stay engaged, and the interface remains readable from several meters away.

## Brand Personality

Playful, encouraging, classroom-friendly. The interface should feel like an original campus learning game: bright, rounded, tactile, optimistic, and classroom-friendly without becoming visually chaotic.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing pages, dark AI gradients, glass cards, tiny marketing labels, complex dashboards, realistic stock photography, and decorative effects that distract from classroom instruction. Avoid fake app-store links or product claims that are not part of the classroom tool.

## Design Principles

1. Projector-first clarity: every primary instruction, answer, and navigation control must be readable at classroom distance.
2. One facilitator, many learners: controls should be obvious to the operator while visual feedback is big enough for the children.
3. Campus-learning tactility: primary actions use academic blue, rounded shapes, chunky shadows, and responsive press states.
4. Encouragement over pressure: feedback copy, mascot states, and reward moments should feel supportive.
5. Keep the flow manual and predictable: no auto-advancing lessons or hidden state changes that could disrupt a live session.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target high contrast, large text, 44px minimum touch targets, keyboard focus visibility, and reduced-motion alternatives for non-essential animation. The app is optimized for Chrome and Edge on a laptop connected to a projector at 1280x720 or larger. Full keyboard-only navigation is not a primary MVP requirement, but interactive controls should remain semantically accessible.
